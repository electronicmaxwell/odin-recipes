Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is nothing short of a culinary masterpiece, a dish that embodies comfort, warmth, and the pure joy of eating. Imagine the moment you cut into a freshly baked lasagna: the layers of tender pasta, perfectly cooked, give way to a symphony of flavors. The rich, meaty sauce, simmered with aromatic garlic, onions, and herbs, melds beautifully with the tangy sweetness of tomatoes, creating a depth of flavor that’s both hearty and satisfying. And then there's the cheese—oh, the cheese! Creamy ricotta, stretchy mozzarella, and sharp Parmesan combine in a luscious, gooey layer that melts into every bite, making each mouthful a celebration of indulgence.

What makes lasagna truly special is how all these elements come together in perfect harmony. Each layer contributes something unique: the pasta provides a tender structure, the sauce infuses every bite with robust flavor, and the cheese ties it all together with its rich, comforting texture. Baked to a golden perfection, with crispy edges and bubbling cheese, lasagna is the ultimate comfort food, offering a warm embrace on a plate. Whether shared with family around a dinner table or enjoyed as leftovers that seem to taste even better the next day, lasagna is more than just a meal—it's an experience, a reminder of the simple yet profound pleasure of good food.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound sweet Italian sausage
  • ¾ pound lean ground beef
  • ½ cup minced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 (6.5 ounce) cans canned tomato sauce
  • 2 (6 ounce) cans tomato paste
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, divided
  • 1 ½ teaspoons dried basil leaves
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, divided, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • ½ teaspoon fennel seeds
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • ¾ pound mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook 1 pound of sweet Italian sausage and ¾ pound of lean ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Add ½ cup minced onion and 2 cloves of crushed garlic to the meat mixture, and sauté until the onion is translucent.
  4. Stir in 1 (28 ounce) can of crushed tomatoes, 2 (6.5 ounce) cans of canned tomato sauce, 2 (6 ounce) cans of tomato paste, and ½ cup water. Mix well.
  5. Season the sauce with 2 tablespoons of white sugar, 4 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley (divided), 1 ½ teaspoons of dried basil leaves, 1 ½ teaspoons of salt (divided, or to taste), 1 teaspoon of Italian seasoning, ½ teaspoon of fennel seeds, and ¼ teaspoon of ground black pepper. Simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook 12 lasagna noodles in the boiling water for 8 to 10 minutes. Drain the noodles and rinse them with cold water. Set aside.
  7. In a mixing bowl, combine 16 ounces of ricotta cheese with 1 egg, the remaining 2 tablespoons of parsley, and ½ teaspoon of salt. Mix until well blended.
  8. To assemble the lasagna, spread 1 ½ cups of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  9. Place 6 lasagna noodles over the sauce. Spread half of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles, then top with a third of the mozzarella cheese slices (about ¼ pound).
  10. Spoon 1 ½ cups of meat sauce over the mozzarella, and sprinkle with ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese.
  11. Repeat the layers, starting with the noodles, then ricotta mixture, mozzarella, and meat sauce, and ending with a final layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  12. Cover the dish with aluminum foil, ensuring the foil does not touch the cheese to prevent sticking. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
  13. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, until the top is golden and bubbly.
  14. Let the lasagna cool for about 15 minutes before serving, allowing the layers to set and making it easier to cut and serve.

Et Voila!
